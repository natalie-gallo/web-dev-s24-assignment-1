The Seahorse

The Seahorse (Hippocampus) are tiny fishes that swim in tropical coastal waters. The measure in size between a half an inch to more than 1 foot! They are mainly found between Southern Asia and Austrailia living from 1 to 5 years.

Female seahorses can lay hundreds of eggs at one time. They take up to 45 days to hatch. Their diet is mostly canrivorous, eating crustaceans. There more than 50 species of seahores in the ocean!
