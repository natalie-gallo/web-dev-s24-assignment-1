The Hammerhead Shark

The Hammerhead Shark (Sphyrnidae) is the ocean's predator that can grow up to 20 feet in length. Living an average lifespan of up to 30 years, they search for fish, crabs, squid, and lobsters to live off of.

It uses its wide, hammer shaped head to pin stingrays to the floor. Hammerhead sharks do not lay eggs, instead they give birth to live young. When they find their prey, they can swim at 25 miles per hour!
