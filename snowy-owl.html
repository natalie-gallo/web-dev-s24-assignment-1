The Snowy Owl

The Snowy Owl (Bubo scandiacus) is an iconic bird of prey inhabiting the Arctic tundra and surrounding regions. Recognized for its striking white plumage, the snowy owl is superbly adapted to its snowy surroundings. With keen eyesight and exceptional hunting skills, it preys on lemmings and other small mammals. Unlike many owl species, snowy owls are diurnal, active during both day and night. Their large size and distinctive appearance make them a symbol of the Arctic ecosystem, and they undertake impressive long-distance migrations in search of prey and suitable nesting sites.
