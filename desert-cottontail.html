The Desert Cottontail

The desert cottontail (Sylvilagus auduboni) is an herbivore that mainly feeds on grass and is coprophagic, meaning they consume their own feces. They are mainly active during the evenings but are usually not seen hunting for food during windy days as it can affect their hearing. Due to their size, the cottontail has many predators, such as snakes and coyotes. Its first course of action when spotting a predator is to freeze in place. If being chased, they can hop away in a zig-zag motion, with a speed of over 30km/h.
