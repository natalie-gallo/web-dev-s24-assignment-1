The Kangaroo Rat

The kangaroo rat (Dipodomys) gets their name from the way they hop similar to regular kangaroos. Their fur-lined cheek pouches allow them to store food. Kangaroo rats' diet mostly consists of seeds but will also extend to insects or vegetation. In addition, they are locomotives, meaning they only use their hind legs to hop from 7-9 feet, reaching speeds up to 10km/h.
